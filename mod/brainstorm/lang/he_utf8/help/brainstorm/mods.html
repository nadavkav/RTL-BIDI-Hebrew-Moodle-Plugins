Brainstorming

    Brainstorming is useful in the initial stages of a task, for example for gathering a range of ideas or finding out what students already know collectively.

    It's useful to set some ground rules for brainstorming, which can include:

    1. Everyone's contribution is valued, and no comment or criticism is given until all the contributions are listed;
    2. Everyone has the option to participate, or not;
    3. Wild ideas are as welcome as sober ones in the ideas-generating stage;
    4. Everyone's idea must be expressed in a single sentence or using keywords.
